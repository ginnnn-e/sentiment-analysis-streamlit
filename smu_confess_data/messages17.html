#advice🗣️

If you're having some difficulty in Group Projects, here are some little pieces of advice

1) Most group projects really matter alot less than you think, just deliver something that is acceptable and you'll tend to get an average mark. Do not burn personal studying time for projects.

2) Its alot easier to convince people to do work if you give them super detailed instructions. Think like how the best managers are for interns, essentially breaking down tasks into ultra bite sized pieces of information. (E.g., imagine if the groupmate just had to tackle his/her portion just by filling up short answer questions etc.)

To do this tends to mean someone has to create the entire storyline/structure from the get go. Good skill to hone as well.

3) Sell the vision / some dream. Part of being a great leader is learning to influence / convince people to do their parts. Its honestly much easier to just self-tank something, but when your projects don't matter all that much now (ur job not at stake), why not try brushing up on some management skills.

At the end of the day you'll likely not meet most of these folks ever again. But if you can learn something out of it, that benefits you.
