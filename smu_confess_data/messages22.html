Jensen’s alpha is the avg return on the portfolio over and above that predicted by the CAPM, given the portfolio’s beta and the avg market return

More than just a zesty daddy, we typically use it to risk-adjust and find alpha (the excess returns of an asset relative to market)
This is why the formula is α=rp −[rf+βp*(rm−rf)]
The idea here is that portfolio managers are assessed on their alpha, the added value through active management. If alpha is zero, it means that the portfolio performed in line with the market as predicted by CAPM. negative alpha would mean underperformance on a risk-adjusted benchmark basis

Yes, Jensen's alpha is a zesty daddy that is used to figure out if the portfolio / fund adds more value to you relative to its market exposure
